# TechNova Solutions Pvt. Ltd.

# Email and Cybersecurity Policy

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Department:** Information Technology & Information Security
**Applicable To:** All Employees, Contractors, Interns, Consultants, and Third-Party Users

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Email and Cybersecurity Policy is to establish standards for the secure use of email systems, internet resources, communication platforms, and company information systems. The policy aims to protect TechNova Solutions Pvt. Ltd. against cybersecurity threats including phishing attacks, malware infections, data breaches, ransomware, social engineering attacks, and unauthorized access.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

* Employees
* Contractors
* Consultants
* Interns
* Third-party vendors
* Remote employees
* Hybrid employees

The policy covers:

* Corporate email accounts
* Communication platforms
* Internet access
* Company devices
* Cloud applications
* Mobile devices
* Internal networks

---

# 3. Information Security Objectives

The company seeks to ensure:

* Confidentiality of business information
* Integrity of company systems
* Availability of business services
* Protection against cyber threats
* Compliance with security regulations

---

# 4. Corporate Email Usage

Corporate email accounts are intended for business purposes only.

Employees are permitted to:

* Communicate with customers.
* Collaborate with colleagues.
* Exchange business documents.
* Participate in company activities.

Employees must avoid excessive personal use of company email systems.

---

# 5. Email Security Best Practices

Employees must:

* Verify sender identities.
* Review email addresses carefully.
* Confirm suspicious requests.
* Use professional language.
* Protect confidential information.

Before opening any attachment, employees should verify its legitimacy.

---

# 6. Password Security

Email account passwords must:

* Be at least 12 characters long.
* Include uppercase letters.
* Include lowercase letters.
* Include numbers.
* Include special characters.

Passwords must:

* Never be shared.
* Be changed every 90 days.
* Not contain personal information.
* Not be reused.

---

# 7. Multi-Factor Authentication

MFA is mandatory for:

* Corporate email accounts
* Administrative accounts
* Cloud platforms
* Remote access systems

Approved MFA methods include:

* Authentication applications
* Hardware security keys
* SMS verification codes

---

# 8. Phishing Awareness

Phishing attacks attempt to steal sensitive information through deceptive communications.

Common indicators include:

* Urgent requests
* Suspicious links
* Unknown senders
* Unexpected attachments
* Grammar mistakes
* Requests for passwords

Employees should never provide credentials through email.

---

# 9. Social Engineering Attacks

Examples of social engineering attacks include:

* Phishing
* Spear phishing
* Vishing
* Smishing
* Impersonation attacks
* Business email compromise

Employees must verify unusual requests through alternative communication channels.

---

# 10. Attachment Security

Employees should:

* Scan attachments before opening.
* Avoid opening suspicious files.
* Verify file extensions.
* Report unusual attachments.

High-risk file types include:

* .exe
* .bat
* .cmd
* .vbs
* .scr
* .js

---

# 11. Link Verification

Before clicking links, employees should:

* Hover over URLs.
* Verify domain names.
* Check HTTPS certificates.
* Confirm sender legitimacy.

Employees should avoid clicking shortened URLs from unknown sources.

---

# 12. Internet Usage Policy

Employees may use company internet resources for authorized business purposes.

Employees must not:

* Access illegal websites.
* Download pirated software.
* Visit malicious websites.
* Conduct unauthorized activities.
* Bypass security controls.

Internet activity may be monitored.

---

# 13. Malware Protection

Company systems are protected using:

* Antivirus software
* Endpoint protection
* Firewalls
* Threat detection systems
* Security monitoring tools

Employees must not disable security software.

---

# 14. Ransomware Protection

Employees should:

* Avoid suspicious attachments.
* Maintain updated systems.
* Report unusual behavior.
* Store files on approved servers.
* Follow backup procedures.

In case of ransomware infection:

* Disconnect the device immediately.
* Contact IT security.
* Do not pay ransom demands.

---

# 15. Data Classification

Company information is classified as:

| Classification | Description                     |
| -------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Public         | Information available publicly  |
| Internal       | Internal company information    |
| Confidential   | Sensitive business information  |
| Restricted     | Highly confidential information |

Employees must protect information according to its classification.

---

# 16. Data Loss Prevention

Employees must not:

* Email confidential information externally without approval.
* Store sensitive information on personal devices.
* Use unauthorized cloud storage services.
* Share confidential information through personal email accounts.

---

# 17. Mobile Device Security

Mobile devices accessing company resources must:

* Use passcodes.
* Enable encryption.
* Install security updates.
* Enable remote wipe functionality.
* Use approved applications only.

Lost devices must be reported immediately.

---

# 18. Remote Work Security

Remote employees must:

* Use company VPN.
* Connect through secure networks.
* Lock devices when unattended.
* Avoid public computers.
* Protect company information.

Remote workers are responsible for maintaining secure work environments.

---

# 19. Security Incident Reporting

Examples of reportable incidents include:

* Phishing emails
* Malware infections
* Unauthorized access
* Lost devices
* Data breaches
* Credential theft

Employees must report incidents within one hour of discovery.

---

The incident response process includes:

Detection.

### Step 2

Containment.

### Step 3

Investigation.

### Step 4

Recovery.

### Step 5

Documentation.

### Step 6

Prevention improvements.

All employees must complete:

* Cybersecurity awareness training
* Phishing simulation exercises
* Password management training
* Data privacy training
* Compliance training

Training occurs quarterly.

---

# 22. Monitoring and Auditing

The company may monitor:

* Email activity
* Network traffic
* Login attempts
* Internet usage
* Security events
* Device activity

Monitoring is performed to protect business assets and ensure compliance.

---

# 23. Policy Violations

Violations include:

* Sharing passwords
* Clicking malicious links intentionally
* Unauthorized software installation
* Bypassing security controls
* Mishandling confidential information

Possible disciplinary actions include:

* Verbal warning
* Written warning
* Suspension
* Termination
* Legal action

---

# 24. Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1. Can employees use personal email for company business?

No. Only company-approved email systems may be used.

### Q2. What should I do if I receive a suspicious email?

Do not click any links or attachments. Report it immediately.

### Q3. Is MFA mandatory?

Yes. MFA is required for all critical systems.

### Q4. Can internet activity be monitored?

Yes. Company internet usage may be monitored.

### Q5. What happens if a device is lost?

The employee must immediately notify IT and Information Security.
