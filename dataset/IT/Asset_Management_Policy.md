# TechNova Solutions Pvt. Ltd.

# IT Asset Management Policy

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Department:** Information Technology
**Applicable To:** All Employees, Contractors, Consultants, and Third-Party Users

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this IT Asset Management Policy is to establish standardized procedures for the procurement, allocation, maintenance, tracking, security, and disposal of information technology assets within TechNova Solutions Pvt. Ltd.

This policy ensures efficient utilization of IT resources, reduces operational risks, maintains regulatory compliance, and protects company investments.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to all technology assets owned, leased, or managed by TechNova Solutions Pvt. Ltd., including:

* Desktop computers
* Laptop computers
* Mobile devices
* Servers
* Network equipment
* Storage devices
* Printers
* Software licenses
* Cloud resources
* Security devices
* Peripherals

The policy applies to:

* Employees
* Contractors
* Consultants
* Interns
* Third-party vendors

---

# 3. Definitions

## IT Asset

Any hardware, software, cloud resource, or information technology component that provides business value.

## Asset Owner

The department or individual responsible for the usage and management of an assigned asset.

## Asset Lifecycle

The complete lifecycle of an asset, from acquisition to disposal.

# 6. Asset Procurement

All IT assets must be procured through approved procurement procedures.

The procurement process includes:

1. Business requirement submission.
2. Budget approval.
3. Vendor selection.
4. Purchase authorization.
5. Asset registration.
6. Asset deployment.

Unauthorized purchases are prohibited.

---

# 7. Asset Registration

Every asset must be registered in the IT Asset Management System.

Required asset information includes:

* Asset ID
* Asset category
* Serial number
* Model number
* Purchase date
* Warranty information
* Assigned employee
* Department
* Location
* Status

---

# 8. Asset Ownership

The company retains ownership of all IT assets.

Employees are considered custodians of assigned equipment and are responsible for:

* Proper usage
* Physical protection
* Security compliance
* Timely reporting of issues
* Returning assets when requested

---

# 9. Asset Allocation

Asset allocation is based on:

* Employee role
* Business requirements
* Department standards
* Security requirements

Typical allocations include:

| Employee Type     | Standard Assets                     |
| ----------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Software Engineer | Laptop, Monitor, VPN Access         |
| Manager           | Laptop, Mobile Device               |
| Data Analyst      | Laptop, Monitor, Analytics Software |
| Intern            | Laptop, Limited Software Access     |

---

# 10. Asset Tracking

The IT department maintains continuous tracking of:

* Asset location
* Asset owner
* Asset condition
* Warranty status
* Software licenses
* Maintenance history

Periodic audits are conducted to ensure accuracy.

---

# 11. Hardware Asset Management

All hardware assets must:

* Be tagged with company asset numbers.
* Be inventoried regularly.
* Receive periodic maintenance.
* Follow warranty procedures.
* Be secured against theft and damage.

Employees must report hardware issues immediately.

---

# 12. Software Asset Management

Software installations require prior approval.

The company manages:

* Software licenses
* License renewals
* Software inventory
* Compliance audits
* Version management

Unauthorized software installation is prohibited.

---

# 13. Cloud Asset Management

Cloud resources must:

* Be approved by IT.
* Follow security standards.
* Be monitored regularly.
* Have documented ownership.
* Comply with budget controls.

Cloud environments include:

* AWS
* Microsoft Azure
* Google Cloud Platform

---

# 14. Security Requirements

All IT assets must comply with security requirements including:

* Antivirus protection
* Disk encryption
* Firewall configuration
* Security updates
* Endpoint monitoring
* Access controls

Security controls may not be disabled without authorization.

---

# 15. Asset Maintenance

Regular maintenance activities include:

* Software updates
* Security patching
* Hardware servicing
* Performance optimization
* Backup verification
* Warranty servicing

Maintenance schedules are managed by the IT department.

---

# 16. Asset Audits

IT asset audits are conducted:

| Audit Type     | Frequency |
| -------------- | --------- |
| Hardware Audit | Quarterly |
| Software Audit | Quarterly |
| Security Audit | Monthly   |
| License Audit  | Annually  |

Employees must cooperate during audit activities.

---

# 17. Lost or Stolen Assets

Employees must report lost or stolen assets immediately.

The reporting process includes:

1. Notify manager.
2. Notify IT Helpdesk.
3. Notify Information Security.
4. Submit incident report.
5. Participate in investigation.

Delayed reporting may result in disciplinary action.

---

# 18. Asset Return Procedures

Employees must return company assets during:

* Resignation
* Termination
* Department transfer
* Equipment replacement
* Long-term leave

Returned assets include:

* Laptops
* Mobile devices
* ID cards
* Security tokens
* Peripherals

---

# 19. Asset Disposal

Asset disposal must follow approved procedures.

Disposal methods include:

* Secure destruction
* Recycling
* Vendor buyback
* Donation programs

Before disposal:

* All company data must be securely erased.
* Security certificates must be revoked.
* Asset records must be updated.

---

# 20. Environmental Responsibility

The company supports environmentally responsible disposal practices.

Employees and vendors must comply with:

* Electronic waste regulations
* Recycling standards
* Environmental policies

---

# 21. Vendor Management

Vendors providing IT assets must:

* Meet company standards.
* Provide warranties.
* Offer support agreements.
* Maintain regulatory compliance.
* Protect company information.

Vendor performance is reviewed annually.

---

# 22. Policy Violations

Examples of violations include:

* Unauthorized software installation.
* Asset misuse.
* Failure to return equipment.
* Unauthorized asset transfer.
* Disabling security controls.
* Asset theft.

Disciplinary actions may include:

* Verbal warning
* Written warning
* Financial recovery
* Suspension
* Termination
* Legal action

# 23. Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1. Who owns company-issued laptops?

TechNova Solutions Pvt. Ltd. retains ownership of all company assets.

### Q2. Can employees install personal software?

No. Only approved software installations are permitted.

### Q3. What should employees do if equipment is damaged?

Employees must immediately report the issue to the IT Helpdesk.

### Q4. Are software licenses monitored?

Yes. Software licenses are regularly audited.

### Q5. What happens if company assets are not returned?

The company may initiate recovery procedures and disciplinary action.
