# TechNova Solutions Pvt. Ltd.

# Performance Review Policy

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Department:** Human Resources
**Applicable To:** All Employees

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of the Performance Review Policy is to establish a structured and transparent framework for evaluating employee performance, recognizing achievements, identifying development opportunities, and supporting career growth within TechNova Solutions Pvt. Ltd.

This policy ensures fairness, consistency, and alignment between employee contributions and organizational goals.

---

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

* Full-time employees
* Permanent employees
* Employees who have completed probation
* Hybrid and remote employees

This policy does not apply to interns, consultants, or temporary contract workers unless specified by management.

---

# 3. Objectives of Performance Management

The objectives of the performance review process are:

* Evaluate employee performance objectively.
* Align employee goals with business objectives.
* Recognize and reward outstanding performance.
* Identify skill gaps and development needs.
* Improve employee engagement and productivity.
* Support promotion and compensation decisions.

---

# 4. Performance Review Cycle

Performance evaluations are conducted twice every year.

| Review Type     | Period          |
| --------------- | --------------- |
| Mid-Year Review | January – June  |
| Annual Review   | July – December |

Special performance reviews may be conducted if required by business needs.

# 6. Performance Rating Scale

The company uses a five-point rating scale.

| Rating | Description          |
| ------ | -------------------- |
| 5      | Outstanding          |
| 4      | Exceeds Expectations |
| 3      | Meets Expectations   |
| 2      | Needs Improvement    |
| 1      | Unsatisfactory       |

---

## Rating Definitions

### Rating 5: Outstanding

Employees consistently exceed expectations and demonstrate exceptional performance.

### Rating 4: Exceeds Expectations

Employees perform above expected standards and contribute significantly.

### Rating 3: Meets Expectations

Employees successfully fulfill assigned responsibilities.

### Rating 2: Needs Improvement

Employees require additional support and development.

### Rating 1: Unsatisfactory

Performance is significantly below organizational expectations.

---

# 7. Goal Setting Process

At the beginning of each review cycle:

Employees and managers jointly establish goals based on:

* Business objectives
* Project requirements
* Professional development goals
* Department priorities

Goals should follow the SMART framework:

* Specific
* Measurable
* Achievable
* Relevant
* Time-bound

---

# 8. Performance Review Process

The performance evaluation process consists of the following steps.

## Step 1: Goal Definition

Employees and managers define objectives.

## Step 2: Self-Assessment

Employees evaluate their own performance.

## Step 3: Manager Evaluation

Managers assess employee performance.

## Step 4: Review Meeting

Employee and manager discuss performance.

## Step 5: Final Rating

Final performance ratings are assigned.

## Step 6: Development Planning

Future goals and improvement plans are established.

---

# 12. Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

Employees receiving ratings below expectations may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan.

## Objectives of PIP

* Address performance gaps
* Provide coaching
* Establish measurable goals
* Monitor progress

---

## Standard PIP Duration

| Stage             | Duration |
| ----------------- | -------- |
| Initial Review    | 30 Days  |
| Monitoring Period | 60 Days  |
| Final Evaluation  | 90 Days  |

---

## Possible Outcomes

* Successful completion
* Extension of PIP
* Role reassignment
* Termination of employment

# 17. Appeals Process

Employees who disagree with their performance rating may:

1. Discuss concerns with their manager.
2. Request review by Human Resources.
3. Submit a formal appeal.

All appeals are reviewed fairly and confidentially.

---

# 18. Confidentiality

Performance evaluations are confidential and accessible only to:

* Employees
* Reporting managers
* Human Resources
* Authorized leadership personnel

Unauthorized disclosure is prohibited.

---

# 19. Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1. How often are performance reviews conducted?

Twice per year.

### Q2. Can employees challenge their ratings?

Yes, through the formal appeals process.

### Q3. Are bonuses linked to performance?

Yes, bonuses are partially determined by performance ratings.

### Q4. Can employees receive promotions during the year?

Yes, subject to business needs and exceptional performance.

### Q5. Is self-assessment mandatory?

Yes, employees are required to complete self-assessments.
